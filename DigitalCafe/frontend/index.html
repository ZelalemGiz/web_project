From Green Bean to Your Cup

Discover the art and science behind every perfect cup at Bean Haven. Our master roasters follow this traditional 5-step process to unlock the deepest flavors.

01

1. Green Bean Arrival

Fresh, unroasted beans from Ethiopia’s finest farms arrive weekly.

These beans are hand-selected for quality and moisture content. We store them in a climate-controlled room to preserve freshness before roasting begins.

Key Temperature: Room temperature (18–22°C)
Duration: Inspection & resting (1–2 days)
02

2. Preheating & Loading

Our drum roaster is preheated to the perfect starting temperature.

Beans are loaded into the preheated roaster. Hot air begins to circulate, gently removing any remaining moisture from the green beans.

Key Temperature: 180–200°C
Duration: 4–6 minutes (drying phase)
03

3. First Crack

The magic moment — beans expand and produce the famous “first crack” sound.

This is when the Maillard reaction begins. Sugars and amino acids create hundreds of new flavor compounds. We listen carefully to control the roast profile.

Key Temperature: 205–210°C
Duration: 8–10 minutes total roast time
04

4. Development & Cooling

Flavor is locked in, then beans are rapidly cooled to stop the roast.

We decide the exact moment to drop the beans based on desired roast level (light, medium, or dark). Immediate cooling preserves the delicate aromas.

Key Temperature: Drop at 220–240°C
Duration: 1–2 minutes cooling
05

5. Resting & Packaging

Beans rest for 24–48 hours before being packed fresh for you.

CO₂ is released during resting, which improves taste. Finally, the beans are sealed in our signature bags with a one-way valve to keep them at peak freshness.

Key Temperature: Room temperature
Duration: 24–48 hours resting
Every roast is logged with temperature curves so we can consistently deliver the exact flavor profile you love.
